Assignment 1 — Luni App

Q1: What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?
I noticed the parallax movement in the 3D scene as the mouse moved, creating spatial immersion and making the interface feel interactive and alive.
Q2: Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.
Mouse movement, hover effects, scroll-triggered transitions, button clicks, 3D object interactions (like shooting, dragging, toggling), and dynamic scene replacements based on thumbnail clicks.
Q3: What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?
The interactive 3D objects — especially the mini-games like basketball/soccer scenes — where clicking and feedback made me want to experiment repeatedly.
Q4: What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?
Mouse hover and scrolling were the most repeated actions — used to navigate scenes and reveal microinteractions.
Q5: What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?
The goal is immersive brand storytelling and exploration — using interactivity to enhance user engagement and deliver information in a playful way.
Q6: How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?
It uses dynamic visual feedback, 3D animations, scroll effects, and reactive UI components to encourage discovery and play.
Q7: What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?
Designed for short but repeatable interactions — first visit for exploration (3–5 mins), return visits to specific sections like app previews or team info.
Q8: How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?
It uses scroll-reveal structures, expandable content, and replayable elements (games, rewards) to suggest that it's worth coming back to.
Q9: What other media forms does the experience reference?
Video games (UI/UX), interactive 3D sites (like Apple Vision Pro), and immersive brand installation concepts (experience centers).
Q10: What does this reference communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with the experience?
It tells me to interact actively, like a player in a game — try clicking, dragging, exploring, and even "breaking" elements to find more.
Q11: What does this reference communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with the experience?
Curious, playful, immersed — like exploring a fun and beautifully designed playground where discovery is rewarded.
Q12: What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?
Some interactive objects are not clearly signposted as clickable. The transitions are sometimes too long or unskippable, which slows down flow.
Q13: What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?
The mini-game elements (like shooting/goal scoring) and responsive UI transitions made the experience engaging and visually rewarding.
